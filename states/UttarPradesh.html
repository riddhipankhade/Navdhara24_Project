The Parks of Uttar Pradesh

The Famous National Parks In Uttar Pradesh

1.Dudhwa National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available at various entry points and within the park.

Cafes: There are no dedicated cafes within the park; however, some nearby towns may offer food options.

Clinics: Medical facilities are limited; it's advisable to carry basic medical supplies and consult with local guides for emergencies.

Hotels Available

In-Park Accommodations: Forest lodges and guesthouses operated by the Uttar Pradesh Forest Department offer basic amenities. Some options include:

  • Kishanpur Range Forest Rest House
  • Dudhwa Lodge
  • Bijrani Forest Rest House

Nearby Hotels: There are several hotels and lodges in nearby towns such as Palia Kalan and Lakhimpur Kheri:

  • Hotel Sun City, Lakhimpur Kheri
  • Hotel Shubham, Palia Kalan
  • Hotel J.P. Palace, Lakhimpur Kheri

Events Planned

Events: The park hosts periodic wildlife awareness programs, guided tours, and educational workshops. Specific event details may vary annually.

Activities Done

Wildlife Safari: Explore the park on guided jeep safaris or elephant rides.

Bird Watching: Dudhwa is a birdwatcher’s paradise with over 400 bird species.

Nature Walks: Guided nature walks are available for a closer look at flora and fauna.

Photography: The park offers excellent opportunities for wildlife and landscape photography.

What It Is Famous For

Famous For: Dudhwa National Park is renowned for its population of the endangered One-Horned Rhinoceros, its diverse wildlife, and rich biodiversity.

Animals It Is Famous For

Notable Animals: One-Horned Rhinoceros, Bengal Tiger, Swamp Deer, Leopard, Elephant, Various bird species, including the endangered Pallas's Fish Eagle

Plants/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park features a mix of tropical moist deciduous forests, tall grasslands, and swampy areas.

Climate: Dudhwa experiences a subtropical climate with hot summers, moderate winters, and a monsoon season from June to September.

Restaurants Available

On-site Dining: Limited. Visitors typically need to rely on nearby towns for restaurant services. Some options include local eateries in Palia Kalan and Lakhimpur Kheri.

Recommended Activities

Safari Tours: Ideal for spotting the park’s diverse wildlife.

Bird Watching: Particularly rewarding due to the park's rich avian diversity.

Nature Photography: Capture the scenic beauty and wildlife of Dudhwa.

Exploring Grasslands: Witness unique species that thrive in the grassland habitats.

Cultural Visits: Engage with local communities and learn about conservation efforts.
