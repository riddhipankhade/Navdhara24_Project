The Parks of Rajasthan

The Famous National Parks In Rajasthan

1.Desert National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic facilities are available at the park's main entry points and some visitor centers.

Cafes: Limited, as the park is quite remote. It’s advisable to carry your own food and water.

Clinics: There are no medical facilities within the park. It’s best to carry a first-aid kit and be prepared for emergencies. Nearest hospitals are in Jaisalmer.

Hotels Available

Luxury: Suryagarh: A luxury hotel with a desert experience.
The Serai: Offers luxury tents and desert experiences.

Mid-Range: Hotel Rang Mahal: A comfortable stay with modern amenities.
Hotel Fort Rajwada: Provides a traditional Rajasthani experience.

Budget: Desert Boys Guest House: Budget accommodation with basic amenities.
Shree Ram International: Offers budget-friendly options with essential facilities.

Events Planned

Desert Festival: Held annually in February, showcasing traditional Rajasthani music, dance, and cultural performances.

Camel Festival: Also held in Jaisalmer, featuring camel races, cultural performances, and more.

Activities

Camel Safaris: Experience the desert landscape and local culture.

Sand Dune Exploration: Explore the vast dunes, especially at Sam and Khuri.

Bird Watching: Particularly during the migratory season, various bird species can be spotted.

Photography: The desert landscape and traditional architecture offer excellent photography opportunities.

What It Is Famous For

Unique Desert Ecosystem: The park is famous for its rare desert flora and fauna, and its stark, beautiful desert landscape.

Famous Animal

Great Indian Bustard: One of the most iconic and endangered species found in the park.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: Sparse, with hardy desert plants like cacti, acacias, and grasses.

Climate: Extremely arid, with scorching temperatures in summer and cool nights. Winter temperatures can be quite pleasant.

Restaurants

Within the Park: There are limited dining options within the park. Most visitors rely on hotels or carry their own food and water.

Nearby Options: Nearby towns like Jaisalmer offer more dining choices.

Recommended Activities

Camel Safaris: To explore the desert and experience local life.

Sunset Viewing: The sunset over the sand dunes is breathtaking.

Visit Kuldhara Village: An abandoned village with an interesting history and architecture.

Cultural Experiences: Engage with local communities and enjoy traditional Rajasthani music and dance.

2.Keoladeo National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Available at various points within the park.

Cafes: There are a few basic refreshment stalls within the park, but options are limited. For better dining options, you might need to venture outside the park.

Clinics: Basic medical facilities are available near the park's entrance; however, for more serious medical needs, you might need to go to nearby towns.

Hotels Available

Inside the Park: Limited accommodation options are available within the park itself.

Nearby: There are several hotels and lodges in the nearby town of Bharatpur, such as:
The Bagh: A heritage hotel offering comfortable stay options.
Hotel Sunbird: A budget-friendly option.
Laxmi Vilas Palace: A more luxurious choice.

Events Planned

Bird Watching Festivals: The park often hosts bird watching festivals and events, especially during the migratory season.

Photography Workshops: Occasionally, workshops and guided photography tours are organized.

Activities

Bird Watching: The park is a paradise for bird watchers, with over 370 species recorded.

Boating: Available on the park's main lake.

Cycling: Bicycles can be rented to explore the park's trails.

Nature Walks: Guided walks and tours are available to enhance the experience.

What It Is Famous For

Bird Sanctuary: It’s famous for being one of the most important bird breeding and feeding grounds in India.

Migratory Birds: The park attracts numerous migratory birds, especially during the winter months.

Famous Animal

Sarus Crane: Keoladeo is particularly renowned for its population of the Sarus Crane, one of the largest flying birds.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park features a mix of dry deciduous forests, grasslands, and wetlands. Common vegetation includes acacia, babul, and various aquatic plants.

Climate: The climate varies from hot and dry in the summer to cool and moist in the winter. It’s best to visit from October to March when the weather is pleasant and bird activity is at its peak.

Restaurants Available

Inside the Park: Limited options with basic refreshments.

Nearby: Several dining options in Bharatpur, including:
Shree Nandhini Restaurant: Offers a range of Indian and local cuisine.
Baba Falafel: A casual spot for snacks and light meals.
Mongas Restaurant: Known for traditional Indian meals.

Recommended Activities

Bird Watching Tours: Engage in guided bird watching tours to maximize your experience.

Photography: Capture the diverse avian life and picturesque landscapes.

Nature Walks: Explore the park’s natural beauty and spot various flora and fauna.

Boat Rides: Enjoy a peaceful boat ride on the lake to see waterfowl and other bird species up close.

3.Mukundara Hills National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available within the park.

Cafes: There are no dedicated cafes inside the park, but local eateries can be found in nearby towns.

Clinics: There are no clinics within the park itself; medical facilities are available in nearby towns like Kota and Bundi.

Hotels Available

Near the Park: There are several hotels and guesthouses in Kota and Bundi. Options range from budget to mid-range accommodations. Notable hotels include:
Hotel Suraj Palace (Kota)
The Rajasthan Hotel (Kota)
Hotel Bundi House (Bundi)
Hadoti Palace (Bundi)

Events Planned

Wildlife Viewing: The park occasionally hosts guided wildlife viewing tours and educational events.

Conservation Programs: The park may organize events related to wildlife conservation and environmental awareness.

Activities

Wildlife Safari: The primary activity is wildlife safaris, where visitors can explore the park’s diverse fauna.

Bird Watching: The park is a good location for birdwatching, especially during migration seasons.

Photography: The park’s scenic landscapes and wildlife offer great opportunities for photography.

What Is It Famous For

Mukundara Hills National Park: It is known for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. It is also famous for being a habitat for the endangered Bengal tiger.

Famous Animal

Bengal Tiger: The park is renowned for its efforts to protect and conserve the Bengal tiger, which is a key species in the park.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park features a mix of dry deciduous forests and grasslands.

Climate: Mukundara Hills experiences a typical semi-arid climate with hot summers, moderate winters, and a monsoon season.

Restaurants Available

In the Park: There are no restaurants within the park.

Nearby: Restaurants can be found in Kota and Bundi, offering a range of local and regional cuisines.

Recommended Activities

Safaris: Explore the park’s diverse wildlife through guided safaris.

Bird Watching: Take advantage of the rich avian diversity for bird watching.

Nature Walks: Enjoy nature walks to experience the park's scenic beauty up close.

Photography: Capture the stunning landscapes and wildlife through photography.
