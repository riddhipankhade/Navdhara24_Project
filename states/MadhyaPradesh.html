The Parks of Madhya Pradesh

The Famous National Parks In Madhya Pradesh

1.Bandhavgarh National Park - District Umaria

Facilities

Restrooms: Available at the main entrance gates and within the park at designated spots.

Cafes: Small cafes and refreshment stalls are available near the entrance gates.

Clinics: Basic medical facilities are available at the park. For more serious medical issues, the nearest hospitals are in Umaria and Tala.

Hotels Available

Luxury Accommodations:

Mahua Kothi

Tree House Hideaway

Syna Tiger Resort

Mid-Range Accommodations:

Tiger Trails Resort

Bandhav Vilas

Nature Heritage Resort

Budget Accommodations:

Hotel Baghvihar

Wild Haven Resort

Events Planned

Wildlife Safaris: Regularly scheduled throughout the year.

Bird Watching Tours: Specific times in the year for migratory bird sightings.

Conservation Workshops: Occasionally held for awareness and education.

Activities Done

Jeep Safaris: The most popular way to explore the park and view wildlife.

Elephant Safaris: Available in some zones for a unique experience.

Trekking: Limited trekking options are available with guides.

Bird Watching: Especially popular during the winter months.

What It Is Famous For

Historical Importance: The park is known for the ancient Bandhavgarh Fort, which dates back 2,000 years.

High Density of Tigers: One of the highest tiger populations in India, making it a prime location for tiger sightings.

Famous Animal

Bengal Tiger: The park is renowned for its Bengal tiger population, providing excellent opportunities for sightings.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park is a mix of tropical forests, including Sal trees, bamboo, and grasslands.

Climate: The climate is tropical with hot summers, monsoon rains, and cool winters.

Restaurants Available

Tiger's Cafe: Near the main entrance, offering local and continental cuisine.

Nature's Feast: Located within a nearby resort, offering a range of dining options.

Jungle Delight: A popular dining spot within a mid-range resort.

Recommended Activities

Morning and Evening Safaris: For the best chances of wildlife sightings.

Bird Watching Excursions: Especially recommended during the winter season.

Visit to Bandhavgarh Fort: A historical site within the park offering scenic views and a glimpse into the region's history.

Photography Tours: Specialized tours for wildlife photographers.

Nature Walks: Guided walks to explore the flora and fauna in detail.

2.Ghughua Fossil National Park - Dindori District

Facilities

Restrooms: Available at the park entrance and key locations within the park.

Cafes: Limited; visitors are advised to carry their own refreshments.

Clinics: No clinics within the park; nearest medical facilities are in Dindori town.

Hotels Available

Nearby Accommodation:

Government guest houses and lodges in Dindori town.

Private hotels and guest houses are also available in Dindori.

Events Planned

Fossil Education Programs: Conducted periodically to educate visitors about the significance of the fossils found in the park.

Guided Tours: Scheduled tours with expert guides are available to provide detailed information about the fossils and the park's history.

Activities Done

Fossil Viewing: The primary activity is exploring and viewing the various fossilized plants and trees.

Photography: Capture images of the unique fossil formations and scenic beauty.

Guided Walks: Learn about the geological history and significance of the area.

What is it Famous For

Fossils: Known for its rich collection of plant and tree fossils dating back to the Mesozoic era, around 65 million years ago.

Famous Animal

No specific animal: The park is primarily known for its fossilized flora rather than fauna.

Famous Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Fossilized Vegetation: Includes fossilized trees, leaves, fruits, and seeds from the Mesozoic era.

Climate: Semi-arid with moderate temperatures, making it suitable for year-round visits.

Restaurants Available

Local Eateries: Small eateries and food stalls are available in Dindori town, offering local cuisine.

Recommended Activities

Educational Tours: Participate in educational tours to gain insight into the ancient plant life.

Nature Walks: Enjoy leisurely walks while exploring the fossilized remains and learning about the park's history.

Photography: Capture the unique and ancient fossil formations.

Picnicking: Designated areas for picnicking while enjoying the natural surroundings.

Additional Tips

Preparation: Carry sufficient water and snacks, as amenities are limited within the park.

Guides: Hiring a local guide can enhance the visit with detailed information and interesting facts.

Weather: Check the weather forecast before planning your visit to ensure a comfortable experience.

Kanha National Park - Mandla District, Balaghat District

Facilities

Restrooms: Available at various points within the park and at the visitor centers.

Cafes and Restaurants: There are eateries near the entrance gates and in the nearby towns.

Clinics: Basic medical facilities and first aid are available within the park. More comprehensive medical services are available in nearby towns.

Visitor Centers: Provide information about the park, wildlife exhibits, and educational programs.

Hotels Available

Luxury Accommodations:

Taj Safari Lodge

Kanha Earth Lodge

Singinawa Jungle Lodge

Mid-range Accommodations:

Mogli Resort

Chitvan Jungle Lodge

Tuli Tiger Resort

Budget Accommodations:

MPT Baghira Jungle Resort

Wild Chalet Resort

Kanha Village Eco Resort

Events Planned

Wildlife Safaris: Morning and evening safaris are scheduled daily.

Nature Walks: Guided walks to explore the flora and fauna.

Conservation Programs: Workshops and lectures on wildlife conservation.

Local Festivals: Occasionally, local cultural events and festivals are celebrated within the park vicinity.

Activities

Jeep Safaris: The most popular activity for wildlife spotting, especially tigers.

Elephant Safaris: Offered for a more unique and traditional wildlife viewing experience.

Bird Watching: Kanha is a bird watcher’s paradise with numerous species.

Photography Tours: Specialized tours for wildlife photography enthusiasts.

Nature Trails: Exploring the park on foot through designated trails.

What It’s Famous For

Breathtaking Landscapes: The park is known for its stunning meadows, dense forests, and tranquil lakes.

Rudyard Kipling's Inspiration: The park inspired the setting for the classic novel "The Jungle Book."

Famous Animals

Bengal Tiger: The park is renowned for its healthy population of Bengal tigers.

Barasingha (Swamp Deer): Kanha is famous for the conservation of the Barasingha, which is also known as the jewel of Kanha.

Leopards, Indian wild dogs (Dholes), and Sloth bears: Other significant wildlife includes leopards, Indian wild dogs, and sloth bears.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Flora: The park features a rich variety of flora, including sal and bamboo forests, grasslands, and meadows.

Vegetation: Predominantly deciduous forests with Sal (Shorea robusta) being the primary tree species.

Climate:

Summer: Hot and dry, with temperatures ranging from 24°C to 42°C.

Monsoon: Heavy rains from July to September.

Winter: Pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 11°C to 25°C.

Restaurants Available

In-Park Eateries: Small cafes and eateries at the main entrance gates.

Nearby Restaurants: Several hotels and resorts have in-house dining options that serve a variety of cuisines, including local and international dishes.

Recommended Activities

Jeep Safari: To get the best chance of spotting tigers and other wildlife.

Elephant Safari: For a unique wildlife experience.

Bird Watching Tours: For enthusiasts keen on spotting various bird species.

Visit to Kanha Museum: To learn more about the park’s history, flora, and fauna.

Nature Trails and Walks: For those who enjoy exploring nature on foot.
