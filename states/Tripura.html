The Parks of Tripura

The Famous National Parks In Tripura

1.Bison (Rajbari) National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic facilities are available within the park, typically at entry points and main areas.

Cafes: There are limited dining options within the park. It’s advisable to carry your own food and water.

Clinics: Medical facilities are minimal within the park. It’s best to have basic first-aid supplies and be prepared to travel to nearby towns for serious medical issues.

Hotels Available

In-Park Lodging: The park does not have in-park lodges or accommodations.

Nearby Options: Several hotels and lodges are available in nearby towns such as Rajbari and other adjacent areas.

Events Planned

Events: Events are typically related to wildlife conservation, nature walks, and guided tours. Specific events vary by season and are best checked with local park authorities or tour operators.

Activities Done

Wildlife Safaris: Jeep safaris and guided nature walks are popular.

Bird Watching: The park is a great spot for bird enthusiasts.

Photography: Ideal for capturing the natural beauty and wildlife.

What It Is Famous For

Wildlife: Known for its population of bison, including the Indian bison or gaur.

Scenic Beauty: The park features rich forests and diverse landscapes.

Notable Animal

Famous Animal: The park is renowned for its population of the Indian bison (Gaur).

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park is characterized by dense forests and diverse flora.

Climate: The climate is tropical, with warm temperatures year-round. The monsoon season brings heavy rains, which enhances the lush greenery.

Restaurants Available

In-Park Dining: Limited, with basic options available.

Nearby: Local eateries and restaurants can be found in nearby towns.

Recommended Activities

Wildlife Viewing: Spotting bison and other wildlife.

Nature Trails: Exploring the forested trails.

Photography: Capturing the park's diverse wildlife and scenic landscapes.

Bird Watching: Observing various bird species in their natural habitat.

2.Clouded Leopard National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restrooms are available at main entry points and visitor centers.

Cafes: Limited food options; you might find basic refreshment stalls near main entry points, but it's recommended to carry your own food and water.

Clinics: There are no dedicated clinics within the park. For medical emergencies, the nearest town or city will have healthcare facilities.

Hotels Available

Within the Park: There are no hotels directly within the park. However, some basic accommodation options may be available at nearby locations.

Nearby: The nearest towns like Shillong and Nongstoin offer a range of accommodation options, from budget hotels to mid-range lodges.

Events Planned

Events: The park doesn't typically host regular events, but local festivals and cultural events in the nearby towns can offer additional experiences.

Activities Done

Wildlife Watching: Spotting the elusive clouded leopard is the primary attraction.

Trekking: There are several trails for trekking and nature walks.

Bird Watching: The park is a great spot for bird enthusiasts.

What It Is Famous For

Famous For: The park is primarily known for being a habitat for the elusive clouded leopard, a rare and endangered big cat species.

Notable Animal

Famous Animal: The park is renowned for the clouded leopard. It's one of the best places in India to potentially see these rare cats in their natural habitat.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park is covered with lush subtropical forests and rich biodiversity, including a variety of flora like orchids and ferns.

Climate: It has a subtropical climate with heavy rainfall during the monsoon season, which contributes to its dense vegetation.

Restaurants Available

Within the Park: No dedicated restaurants are available. It's advisable to bring your own food.

Nearby: The nearby towns of Shillong and Nongstoin have a variety of dining options ranging from local cuisine to more international fare.

Recommended Activities

Wildlife Safaris: Participate in guided safaris to increase your chances of spotting the clouded leopard and other wildlife.

Nature Photography: The park offers excellent opportunities for capturing the rich landscapes and wildlife.

Trekking: Explore the park’s trails for a closer look at its diverse ecosystems.

Cultural Exploration: Experience the local culture and traditions in nearby towns.
