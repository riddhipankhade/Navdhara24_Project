The Parks of Ladakh

The Famous National Parks In Ladakh

1.Hemis National Park - Largest national park in India

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available at the entry points and some key locations within the park.

Cafes: Limited food stalls and small cafes can be found near the entry points and nearby villages.

Clinics: There are basic medical facilities and clinics in the nearby villages, but it's advisable to carry a basic first-aid kit.

Hotels Available

Hemis Monastery Guest House: Provides basic accommodation close to the park.

Stok Palace Heritage Hotel: A luxurious option located in Stok village.

Local Homestays: Numerous homestays in nearby villages offer a more authentic experience.

Events Planned

Hemis Festival: An annual event held at the Hemis Monastery in June or July, featuring traditional music, dance, and rituals.

Activities Done

  • Trekking: Various trekking routes, including the Markha Valley Trek.
  • Wildlife Viewing: Opportunities to spot snow leopards, Tibetan wolves, and other wildlife.
  • Bird Watching: The park is home to several bird species.
  • Photography: Scenic landscapes and wildlife offer excellent photography opportunities.

What is it Famous For

Largest National Park in India: Spanning approximately 4,400 sq. km.

Snow Leopards: Known as the "Snow Leopard Capital of the World."

What Animal is it Famous For

Snow Leopard: Hemis National Park is renowned for its population of snow leopards.

What Plant/Vegetation/Climate is it Famous For

Vegetation: Sparse alpine vegetation, including junipers, birches, and several medicinal plants.

Climate: Cold desert climate with harsh winters and moderate summers.

Restaurants Available

Local Cafes and Eateries: Small eateries in nearby villages offering local Ladakhi cuisine.

Monastery Cafeteria: Simple meals provided at the Hemis Monastery.

Recommended Activities

  • Snow Leopard Tracking: Guided tours for snow leopard sightings.
  • Trekking: Markha Valley and other treks.
  • Visiting Monasteries: Exploring Hemis and other nearby monasteries.
  • Cultural Tours: Interacting with local communities and experiencing Ladakhi culture.
  • Bird Watching: Observing the diverse avian life in the park.
