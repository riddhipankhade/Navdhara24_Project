The Parks of Nagaland

The Famous National Parks In Nagaland

1.Intanki National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available within the park.

Cafes: There are no dedicated cafes within the park; however, local food options can be found in nearby towns.

Clinics: Medical facilities are limited within the park. It's advisable to carry a basic medical kit and visit nearby towns for any serious medical needs.

Hotels

Nearby Accommodations: Kohima: The nearest city with a range of accommodations from budget to luxury hotels. Some options include:

  • Hotel Japfu
  • Hotel De Oriental Grand
  • The Heritage
Dimapur: Another nearby city with several hotel options.

Events Planned

Park Events: Intanki National Park may host periodic events like wildlife safaris, guided tours, and nature walks. Specific events can vary, so it's best to check with local tourism offices or the park's administration for current schedules.

Activities

Wildlife Viewing: The park is known for its diverse flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species.

Bird Watching: A good spot for bird enthusiasts, with numerous bird species found in the park.

Trekking and Nature Walks: Enjoy exploring the park's trails and natural beauty.

What It Is Famous For

Unique Flora and Fauna: Intanki National Park is renowned for its rich biodiversity and unique ecosystems.

Conservation Efforts: The park is part of important conservation initiatives for the region's wildlife.

Famous Animal

Blyth’s Tragopan: The park is known for this elusive and endangered pheasant species.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park features a mix of subtropical and temperate forest types, including bamboo groves and mixed deciduous forests.

Climate: The climate is generally subtropical, with a humid monsoon season and cooler temperatures in the winter.

Restaurants Available

Nearby Options: Dining options are limited within the park but available in nearby towns like Kohima and Dimapur.

Recommended Activities

Wildlife Safaris: Explore the park's diverse wildlife and natural landscapes.

Bird Watching: Ideal for observing a variety of bird species, including Blyth’s Tragopan.

Photography: The park's natural beauty and wildlife offer excellent opportunities for photography.

Cultural Visits: Engage with local communities to learn about their traditions and lifestyles.
