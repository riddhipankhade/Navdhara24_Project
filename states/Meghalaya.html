The Parks of Meghalaya

The Famous National Parks In Meghalaya

1.Balphakram National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available at key entry points and visitor centers.

Cafes: There are small cafes and tea stalls near the park entrance and in nearby villages.

Clinics: Basic medical facilities are available in nearby towns and villages. It's advisable to carry a first aid kit.

Hotels Available

Guest Houses and Lodges: There are a few guest houses and lodges in nearby towns such as Baghmara. It is recommended to book in advance as the options are limited.

Events Planned

Local Festivals and Cultural Events: The park occasionally hosts local festivals and cultural events, celebrating the indigenous traditions of the Garo people. Check with local authorities or tour operators for specific dates.

Activities

Wildlife Safaris: Explore the park’s rich biodiversity through guided jeep safaris.

Bird Watching: The park is a paradise for bird watchers with numerous species to observe.

Trekking: Several trekking trails offer a chance to experience the park’s scenic landscapes.

Photography: Capture the stunning vistas and diverse wildlife.

What is it Famous For

Famous For: Balphakram National Park is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, including deep gorges, cliffs, and lush forests.

Famous Animal

Red Pandas: The park is famous for its population of Red Pandas. Other notable species include elephants, tigers, and the rare golden cat.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park is home to tropical and subtropical forests with a variety of flora, including medicinal plants and orchids.

Climate: The region experiences a moderate climate with heavy rainfall during the monsoon season (June to September).

Restaurants Available

Dining Options: Dining options within the park are limited to small eateries and tea stalls. More variety can be found in nearby towns.

Recommended Activities

Nature Walks: Explore the diverse flora and fauna on foot.

Cultural Tours: Learn about the Garo tribes and their traditions.

Cave Exploration: Visit the nearby Siju Caves, famous for their impressive limestone formations.

2.Nokrek National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restrooms are available at the park's entry points and some of the designated visitor areas.

Cafes: There are no dedicated cafes inside the park. Visitors typically bring their own food or visit nearby towns.

Clinics: There are no clinics within the park. For medical emergencies, visitors need to travel to the nearest town, Tura.

Hotels Available

In Tura: Several hotels and guesthouses are available, including the Hotel Blue Hills, Hotel Ladybird, and Hotel Landmark.

In the Vicinity: Limited options closer to the park, with basic accommodations available in nearby villages.

Events Planned

Festivals: Nokrek National Park occasionally hosts events related to local festivals and cultural activities. The timing and specifics of these events may vary annually.

Activities Done

Trekking: Popular trekking routes offer a chance to explore the park’s rich flora and fauna.

Bird Watching: The park is a hotspot for bird enthusiasts, with various species to observe.

Wildlife Safari: Guided tours provide opportunities to see the park's diverse wildlife.

Photography: Scenic landscapes and wildlife provide excellent photography opportunities.

What It Is Famous For

Biodiversity: Nokrek is renowned for its diverse ecosystem, including rare and endemic species of plants and animals.

The Garo Hills: The park is part of the Garo Hills range, known for its lush greenery and scenic beauty.

What Animal It Is Famous For

Red Panda: The park is famous for being a habitat of the endangered Red Panda, which is one of its key attractions.

Other Wildlife: It also houses other species like the hoolock gibbons, clouded leopards, and various birds.

What Plant/Vegetation/Climate It Is Famous For

Flora: Nokrek is known for its rich diversity of plant life, including several endemic species and tropical evergreen forests.

Climate: The climate is subtropical with high humidity and moderate temperatures, experiencing heavy rainfall during the monsoon season.

Restaurants Available

In Tura: A range of local eateries and restaurants are available in Tura, such as The Garden Restaurant and D.C. Restaurant.

Near the Park: Limited dining options are available, so bringing your own food or eating in nearby towns is advisable.

Recommended Activities

Nature Walks: Explore the park’s natural beauty and diverse ecosystems.

Wildlife Spotting: Engage in guided wildlife tours to spot rare species.

Cultural Interaction: Visit local villages and interact with the Garo community to learn about their traditions and way of life.
