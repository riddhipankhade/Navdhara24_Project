The Parks of Arunachal Pradesh

The Famous National Parks In Arunachal Pradesh

1. Mouling National Park - Siang Valley

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available at designated areas within the park.

Cafes: There are no cafes within the park itself, but nearby villages may have small eateries.

Clinics: No clinics inside the park, but there are basic medical facilities in nearby towns and villages. Travelers are advised to carry a first aid kit and essential medicines.

Hotels Available

Accommodation options: Limited and primarily consist of eco-camps, guesthouses, and forest rest houses. Some options include Forest Rest Houses managed by the forest department, eco-camps in nearby villages, and basic guesthouses in towns like Jengging and Yingkiong.

Events Planned

Nature camps: Occasionally hosts nature camps, wildlife awareness programs, and bird-watching events. It is best to check with local tourism offices or the forest department for updated event schedules.

Activities Done

Trekking: Several trekking routes offer varying levels of difficulty, providing an immersive experience in the park’s natural beauty.

Bird Watching: The park is a haven for bird watchers with its diverse avian population.

Wildlife Safari: Organized safaris can help visitors spot the park’s diverse wildlife.

Nature Walks: Guided nature walks are available to explore the rich flora and fauna.

Photography: The park’s scenic beauty and wildlife make it an excellent spot for photography.

What is it Famous For?

Biodiversity: The park is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including a variety of flora and fauna.

Pristine Wilderness: Known for its untouched natural beauty and serene environment.

Famous Animal

Takin: Mouling National Park is famous for the Takin, a rare and unique animal found in the Eastern Himalayas.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park is characterized by dense tropical forests, sub-tropical forests, and alpine vegetation.

Climate: The climate varies from tropical in the lower regions to alpine in higher altitudes. It experiences heavy rainfall during the monsoon season, with cooler temperatures in the higher elevations.

Restaurants Available

Local Eateries: There are no restaurants within the park. However, nearby villages and towns have small eateries offering local cuisine.

Recommended Activities

Trekking and Hiking: Explore the various trekking trails that offer scenic views and wildlife spotting opportunities.

Bird Watching: The diverse bird species make it a paradise for bird enthusiasts.

Wildlife Safari: Experience the thrill of spotting exotic animals in their natural habitat.

Nature Photography: Capture the breathtaking landscapes and diverse wildlife.

Cultural Visits: Visit nearby villages to experience the local culture and traditions.

2. Namdapha National Park - Near Miao

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available at certain points within the park, particularly at the main entrance and near the campsites.

Cafes: Limited food options are available within the park, with some local eateries near the entrance and in nearby villages.

Clinics: There are no clinics within the park. The nearest medical facilities are in Miao town.

Visitor Centers: Information centers providing maps, guides, and other resources are available at the park entrance.

Hotels Available

Forest Rest Houses: Basic accommodation options managed by the Forest Department are available within the park.

Guest Houses: Miao town has a few guest houses and lodges, such as the Namdapha Jungle Camp and Miao Forest Lodge.

Events Planned

Wildlife Safaris: Regularly organized to explore the rich biodiversity.

Bird Watching Tours: Special events focusing on the diverse bird species in the park.

Trekking Expeditions: Organized treks through various trails within the park.

Activities Done

Wildlife Safari: Explore the park’s diverse wildlife through guided safaris.

Bird Watching: Spot numerous bird species, including some rare and endangered ones.

Trekking: Several trails offer varying levels of difficulty for trekking enthusiasts.

Camping: Designated camping sites allow for overnight stays within the park.

What is it Famous For?

Biodiversity: One of the richest areas in terms of biodiversity in India.

Pristine Wilderness: A vast, untouched natural landscape.

Famous Animal

Hoolock Gibbon: The only ape species found in India and a major attraction in the park.

Snow Leopard: Though rare, it is one of the significant species in the park.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park is home to diverse vegetation types, from tropical to temperate forests.

Climate: The climate ranges from tropical in the lower areas to alpine in the higher regions, making it unique in terms of biodiversity.

Restaurants Available

Local Eateries: Near the park entrance and in Miao town, there are local eateries offering traditional Arunachali cuisine.

Recommended Activities

Wildlife Safari: To explore the rich fauna of the park.

Bird Watching: Given the park's diverse avian population.

Trekking: Various trails offer opportunities to explore the park's natural beauty.

Camping: Experience the park’s wilderness by staying overnight in designated camping areas.

Photography: The park’s scenic beauty and diverse wildlife make it a paradise for photographers.
