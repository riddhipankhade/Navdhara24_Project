The Parks of Odisha

The Famous National Parks In Odisha

1.Bhitarkanika National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available at key points within the park.

Cafes: There are limited food options within the park. It’s best to carry your own snacks and water.

Clinics: There are no clinics within the park itself, but basic medical facilities are available in nearby towns like Kendrapara.

Hotels

Nearby Options: Panthanivas: A government-run accommodation offering basic amenities.
Hotel Nilachal: A hotel in Kendrapara offering more comfortable lodging.
Hotel Surya: Another option in Kendrapara with standard facilities.

Events Planned

Seasonal Festivals: Various local festivals and events occur throughout the year, such as the Raja Festival, which is celebrated with traditional fervor.

Activities

Boat Rides: Exploring the mangrove forests and river channels by boat.

Bird Watching: Observing a variety of bird species, especially during migratory seasons.

Wildlife Viewing: Spotting crocodiles, particularly the saltwater crocodile, and other wildlife.

What It Is Famous For

Saltwater Crocodiles: The park is renowned for its population of saltwater crocodiles. It provides one of the best places in India to observe these creatures in their natural habitat.

Notable Animals

Saltwater Crocodiles: The park is especially famous for its saltwater crocodiles.

Olive Ridley Turtles: Nesting sites for these turtles can be found along the coast.

Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park features extensive mangrove forests and mudflats. It is part of the Deltaic region with a rich collection of flora adapted to saline and brackish conditions.

Climate: The region experiences a tropical climate with a hot and humid climate, particularly during the summer months. Monsoon rains are common from June to September.

Restaurants

In Kendrapara: Options include local eateries and small restaurants offering regional cuisine. Food inside the park is minimal, so dining in Kendrapara is recommended before or after park visits.

Recommended Activities

Guided Boat Tours: Essential for exploring the mangroves and observing wildlife.

Bird Watching Tours: Ideal for enthusiasts wanting to see diverse bird species.

Photography: Capturing the unique landscapes and wildlife of the park.

2.Simlipal National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restrooms are available at the park's entrance and at key points within the park.

Cafes: There are no dedicated cafes within the park. Visitors generally carry their own food or rely on nearby accommodations for meals.

Clinics: Basic first aid facilities are available, but it’s advisable to carry personal medical supplies. The nearest major healthcare facilities are outside the park.

Hotels Available

Inside the Park: Limited accommodation options within the park, mostly forest lodges run by the Odisha Tourism Development Corporation (OTDC). Examples include:
Jumbo Forest Lodge
Nature Camp, Simlipal

Outside the Park: Several hotels and guesthouses are available in nearby towns like Baripada. Some options include:
Hotel Laxmi Palace
Hotel Panthanivas
Hotel Bhubaneswar

Events Planned

Park Events: The park does not have regular, scheduled events but may host occasional wildlife awareness programs, conservation workshops, or guided tours.

Activities

Wildlife Safaris: Guided jeep safaris are the main activity, allowing visitors to explore the park's diverse flora and fauna.

Trekking: There are several trekking trails for those interested in exploring the park on foot.

Bird Watching: The park is a good spot for birdwatching, with numerous species inhabiting the area.

What It Is Famous For

Simlipal National Park: Renowned for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. It is a part of the Simlipal Biosphere Reserve and is known for its picturesque landscapes, including waterfalls and rolling hills.

Famous Animals

Bengal Tiger: Simlipal is particularly famous for its population of Bengal tigers.

Indian Elephant: The park also supports a significant population of Indian elephants.

Plants/Vegetation/Climate

Plants/Vegetation: The park features a diverse range of flora, including tropical moist deciduous forests and sal trees. It’s also home to various medicinal plants.

Climate: The climate is tropical, with hot summers, a monsoon season, and mild winters. Average temperatures range from 20°C to 40°C.

Restaurants

Inside the Park: There are no restaurants within the park. Visitors usually rely on nearby accommodations for meals.

Outside the Park: Restaurants in Baripada and other nearby towns offer local and regional cuisine. Some options include:
Hotel Laxmi Palace Restaurant
Shree Restaurant

Recommended Activities

Safari Tours: Exploring the park's diverse wildlife through guided jeep safaris.

Trekking: Enjoying nature trails and trekking routes.

Photography: Capturing the scenic landscapes and wildlife.

Bird Watching: Observing a variety of bird species in their natural habitat.
