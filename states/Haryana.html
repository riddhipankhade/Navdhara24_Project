The Parks of Haryana

The Famous National Parks In Haryana

1. Kalesar National Park - Near Bilaspur (Yamunanagar district), Paonta Sahib

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restrooms are available at certain entry points and campsites within the park.

Cafes: There are no dedicated cafes within the park. Visitors should bring their own food and water.

Clinics: There are no clinics inside the park. The nearest medical facilities are in Bilaspur or Yamunanagar.

Hotels Available

Nearby Options:

  • Hotel Tushar in Bilaspur
  • The Grand in Yamunanagar
  • Hotel Royal in Paonta Sahib
  • Hotel Classic in Bilaspur

Events Planned

Events: Events and activities are usually organized by the Forest Department or local tourism boards. These may include nature walks, wildlife safaris, and educational tours. It’s best to check with local authorities or the park’s official website for the latest events.

Activities

  • Wildlife Viewing: Spot various species of animals and birds.
  • Nature Walks: Explore the diverse flora and fauna.
  • Photography: Capture the natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Bird Watching: The park is known for its bird species.

Famous For

Wildlife: Known for its rich biodiversity and the presence of several endangered species.

Famous Animal

Famous Animal: The park is particularly known for the tiger population and other large mammals such as leopards and elephants.

Plants/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park features tropical deciduous forests with a mix of broadleaf and coniferous trees.

Climate: The climate is typically subtropical, with hot summers, a monsoon season, and mild winters.

Restaurants Available

On-site: There are no restaurants inside the park. Visitors need to plan meals in advance or eat at nearby towns like Bilaspur or Paonta Sahib.

Recommended Activities

  • Safari Rides: For spotting wildlife.
  • Hiking Trails: For experiencing the landscape and vegetation.
  • Bird Watching Tours: Particularly recommended for enthusiasts.
  • Photography Excursions: To capture the beauty of the park.

2. Sultanpur National Park - Near Gurugram

Facilities

Restrooms: Available at the park entrance and near the main areas.

Cafes: There are limited food options inside the park; it’s advisable to bring your own refreshments.

Clinics: Basic medical facilities are not present inside the park, so it’s best to carry a first-aid kit. Nearest clinics are in Gurugram.

Hotels Available

Budget Hotels: Several budget accommodations are available in Gurugram, including options like Hotel Signature, The Fern Residency, and OYO Rooms.

Mid-Range Hotels: You might consider hotels such as The Leela Ambience, Hilton Garden Inn, and DoubleTree by Hilton.

Luxury Hotels: For a more luxurious stay, options include The Oberoi, Trident, and ITC Grand Bharat.

Events Planned

Events: Sultanpur National Park does not have frequent major events but may host birdwatching workshops or guided tours. Checking their official website or contacting the park directly can provide information on any special events during your visit.

Activities

  • Bird Watching: The park is renowned for its avian population and offers excellent bird-watching opportunities.
  • Nature Walks: Guided nature walks are available to explore the park’s diverse habitats.
  • Photography: The park’s scenic beauty and birdlife provide great opportunities for wildlife photography.

Famous For

Birdwatching: Sultanpur National Park is renowned for being a birdwatcher’s paradise, with over 250 species of birds, including migratory species.

Animal Fame

Bird Species: The park is especially famous for its bird species, including the rare and migratory birds like the Siberian Crane, Greater Flamingo, and various species of herons and egrets.

Plants/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park features a variety of vegetation types, including grasslands, wetlands, and scrublands. Key plant species include various grasses and aquatic plants.

Climate: The climate is semi-arid, with hot summers, mild winters, and a monsoon season. The best time to visit is from October to March.

Restaurants Available

Inside the Park: Limited options; it’s advisable to carry your own food.

Nearby in Gurugram: Various dining options including local eateries, fast-food chains, and restaurants offering diverse cuisines.

Recommended Activities

  • Bird Watching: Spend time observing and identifying different bird species.
  • Photography: Capture the beauty of the park and its wildlife.
  • Nature Trails: Enjoy guided walks to learn about the park’s ecosystem.
  • Picnicking: If facilities permit, a picnic in designated areas can be a pleasant activity.
