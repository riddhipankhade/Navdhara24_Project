The Parks of Kerala

The Famous National Parks In Kerala

1.Anamudi Shola National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Limited facilities are available within the park. It’s advisable to plan ahead.

Cafes: There are no cafes within the park. Nearby towns may have dining options.

Clinics: Medical facilities are limited. In case of emergencies, it's best to visit nearby towns like Munnar.

Hotels Available

Munnar: Munnar is the closest major town with several accommodation options ranging from budget to luxury hotels and resorts. Some popular choices include:

  • Tea County Munnar
  • Parakkat Nature Hotels & Resorts
  • Spice Tree Munnar
  • Windermere Estate

Events Planned

Festivals and Local Events: The park doesn’t host many events directly, but the surrounding areas have cultural and local festivals throughout the year, like the Munnar Tea Festival.

Activities

  • Trekking and Hiking: The park offers several trekking trails. The Anamudi Peak, the highest peak in South India, is a popular destination.
  • Wildlife Viewing: Spotting animals such as the Nilgiri Tahr and other wildlife.
  • Bird Watching: The park is home to a variety of bird species, making it a great spot for bird watchers.
  • Nature Walks: Enjoy guided nature walks to explore the park’s rich biodiversity.

What It Is Famous For

Biodiversity: The park is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including rare and endemic species of flora and fauna.

Anamudi Peak: The highest peak in South India is located here.

Famous Animal

Nilgiri Tahr: This endangered mountain goat is one of the park’s most famous residents.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park is known for its shola forests, which are montane rainforests found in the Western Ghats. It also features grasslands and high-altitude shrubs.

Climate: The climate is typically cool and misty, with heavy rainfall during the monsoon season (June to September).

Restaurants Available

Munnar: There are various restaurants in Munnar offering local and international cuisine, such as:

  • Saravana Bhavan
  • Rapsy Restaurant
  • The Panoramic Getaway

Recommended Activities

  • Trekking to Anamudi Peak: A challenging but rewarding hike.
  • Exploring Shola Forests: Enjoying the unique montane forests and their ecosystems.
  • Wildlife Spotting: Keep an eye out for the Nilgiri Tahr and other wildlife.
  • Bird Watching: Look for endemic and migratory bird species.

2.Eravikulam National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available at the park's visitor centers.

Cafes: There are limited food and drink options inside the park. You might find small stalls or refreshment areas, but it’s a good idea to bring your own snacks and water.

Clinics: There are no medical facilities within the park itself. For any medical needs, you would need to visit nearby towns such as Munnar.

Hotels Available

Munnar: The town of Munnar, which is close to the park, offers a range of accommodations from luxury resorts to budget hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Tea County Munnar
  • The Panoramic Getaway
  • Club Mahindra Munnar
  • Spice Tree Munnar

Events Planned

Eravikulam National Park does not typically host large-scale events. However, seasonal activities and special nature programs might be organized. It's best to check with the park authorities or local tourism offices for any upcoming events or special activities.

Activities Done

  • Trekking: Popular treks include the Anamudi Peak trek, which offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Wildlife Viewing: Spotting the Nilgiri Tahr, a rare mountain goat species, is a highlight.
  • Nature Walks: Guided walks through the park provide insight into its flora and fauna.

Famous For

Nilgiri Tahr: The park is renowned for being one of the last remaining habitats of the endangered Nilgiri Tahr.

Scenic Beauty: The park is famous for its picturesque landscapes, including rolling hills and lush green meadows.

Famous Animal

Nilgiri Tahr: The park is particularly famous for this endangered mountain goat, which is a significant draw for wildlife enthusiasts.

Plant/Vegetation/Climate

Vegetation: The park features shola forests, grasslands, and montane forests.

Climate: The climate is typically cool and temperate, with heavy rainfall during the monsoon season.

Restaurants Available

In the Park: Limited options inside the park.

Nearby: Munnar has a variety of dining options, from local Kerala cuisine to international dishes. Some recommended places include:

  • Saravana Bhavan
  • Rapsy Restaurant
  • The Dhaba
  • Hotel Hillview

Recommended Activities

  • Wildlife Spotting: Look out for the Nilgiri Tahr and other wildlife.
  • Trekking and Hiking: Explore trails like the one leading to Anamudi Peak.
  • Photography: Capture the stunning landscapes and unique flora and fauna.
  • Bird Watching: The park is home to various bird species, making it a good spot for bird watchers.

3.Mathikettan Shola National Park

Facilities

Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities are available at the park entrance and key points.

Cafes: There are no cafes within the park. Visitors usually carry their own food and water.

Clinics: Medical facilities are limited. It's best to carry any personal medications and first aid supplies.

Hotels Available

In and Around the Park: Accommodation options are relatively limited near the park. Most visitors stay in nearby towns like Munnar or Peermade.

  • Munnar: Offers a range of options from budget hotels to luxury resorts.
  • Peermade: Also has several hotels and guesthouses.

Events Planned

The park itself does not typically host large events. However, special programs and nature walks may be organized by the Kerala Forest Development Corporation (KFDC) or other local organizations.

Activities Done

  • Trekking: Popular trekking routes offer views of the park's diverse landscapes.
  • Bird Watching: The park is a haven for bird watchers.
  • Wildlife Viewing: Spotting animals in their natural habitat is a key activity.

What is it Famous For

Mathikettan Shola National Park: The park is renowned for its rich biodiversity and serene natural beauty. It is part of the Western Ghats, which are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

What Animal is it Famous For

Nilgiri Tahr and Lion-tailed Macaque: The park is famous for its populations of Nilgiri Tahr and Lion-tailed Macaque. It also hosts various other species like the Indian Bison, Sambar Deer, and a variety of birds.

What Plant/Vegetation/Climate is it Famous For

Vegetation: The park is known for its shola forests, which are montane rainforests found in the Western Ghats. It also features grasslands and evergreen forests.

Climate: The climate is tropical montane, characterized by cool temperatures and high humidity, especially in the monsoon season.

Restaurants Available

There are no restaurants within the park. Visitors usually dine in nearby towns before or after their visit.

Recommended Activities

  • Nature Walks and Treks: Explore the trails and enjoy the park's natural beauty.
  • Bird Watching: Look for endemic bird species.
  • Photography: Capture the park's stunning landscapes and wildlife.
  • Nature Study: Learn about the unique flora and fauna of the region.
